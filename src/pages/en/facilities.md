# Facilities / Methods

The SOMER Group works across tailing pedogenesis, degraded soil rehabilitation, organo-mineral interactions, and elemental biogeochemical processes through an integrated framework that links field experiments, controlled laboratory studies, and molecular- to nano-scale characterization. Our methodological strength relies both on in-house platforms and on long-term collaborations with large-scale facilities, analytical centers, and ecological field stations.

## Synchrotron-Based Methods and Advanced Spectroscopy

We make extensive use of synchrotron facilities and maintain broad collaborations with teams in Canada, Taiwan, Beijing, and Shanghai. Commonly used methods include:

- XAFS for resolving oxidation state, coordination environment, and mineral phase transformation of Fe, Al, Si, and related elements;
- synchrotron FTIR mapping for identifying organic functional group distributions and their spatial coupling with mineral phases;
- STXM for micro- to nano-scale characterization of co-localization patterns between organic matter and minerals.

These approaches allow us to connect elemental speciation, functional-group chemistry, and spatial organization during tailing pedogenesis and carbon stabilization.

## Nano- and Molecular-Scale Characterization

For nano- and molecular-scale analyses, we routinely use:

- NanoSIMS at Tianjin University for micro-scale elemental mapping and organo-mineral interface analysis;
- 12T FTICR-MS at the Research Center for Eco-Environmental Sciences, CAS, for molecular-level characterization of complex organic matter mixtures.

These techniques are especially useful for resolving the formation, fractionation, and stabilization of microbial-derived carbon, mineral-associated organic matter, and chemically distinct molecular fractions in tailings and degraded soils.

## Controlled and Field Experimental Platforms

Our experimental platforms include:

- indoor climate chambers for controlled studies of temperature, moisture, plants, and microbial processes;
- a large automated greenhouse in Wangdu County for larger-scale tailing rehabilitation and soil process simulations;
- long-term collaborations with ecological field stations for monitoring and comparative field experiments.

These facilities help us bridge mechanistic studies with realistic ecological rehabilitation scenarios.

## Shared Analytical Resources

Through collaborations, we have broad access to the following instruments and facilities:

- Bruker Vertex 80v vacuum FTIR spectrometer (30 - 30000 cm-1)
- AFM (Bruker)
- confocal Raman microscopy (Renishaw)
- micro-FTIR (Bruker)
- elemental analyzer (LEEMAN)
- TOC/TN analyzer for solid and liquid samples (multiCN 2100s, Analytik Jena, Germany)
- HPLC (Agilent 1260)
- gas chromatography systems (Shimadzu GC2014 with TCD/FID; Agilent with FID/ECD)
- Picarro water isotope analyzer

These shared resources provide strong support for multi-scale and multi-type sample analysis across our projects.
